Retail Analytics: Global Sales & Strategy Dashboard
📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes the 2011 sales data of a UK-based retailer to identify growth opportunities and optimize the 2012 fiscal forecast. By isolating international trends and high-value customer behavior, this analysis provides a roadmap for logistics scaling and targeted marketing expansion.
📈 Key Insights & Strategic Findings
1. The "Early Holiday" Revenue Surge
Analysis of monthly revenue patterns reveals that customer activity doesn't peak in December, but rather in November.
Trend: A significant upward surge begins in September, reaching its absolute peak in November.
The "Why": Top revenue drivers include Dotcom Postage, Rabbit Night Lights, and Christmas Decoration Kits.
Strategy: Shift marketing spend to "Preparation & DIY Decor" themes in early Q4 and scale logistics capacity by late October to meet the "Early Holiday" demand.
2. International Market Powerhouses
By excluding the UK (domestic volume) to focus on export growth, the data highlights a dominant "Northern European Corridor."
Market Leader: The Netherlands is the standout international performer, moving over 200,000 units.
Efficiency Check: High volume in the Netherlands suggests a strong B2B/Wholesale interest. Comparing this volume against margins in Germany and France helps identify where shipping costs are most efficient.
Strategy: Cross-promote high-volume products from the Netherlands into the German and EIRE markets to replicate success.
3. Customer Concentration (The "Whale" Analysis)
The "Top 10" highest-value accounts reveal a high concentration of revenue within a few key clients.
Top Performer: Customer ID 14646 is the most critical account, generating approximately $250,000 in revenue alone.
Risk Assessment: The "Waterfall" decline in the chart shows that the top three customers represent a significant portion of international stability.
Strategy: Implement a VIP Loyalty Program and assigned account management for these top 10 IDs to secure nearly $1M in recurring annual revenue.
4. Expansion Roadmap (Global Demand)
Using a horizontal demand chart, we visualized "Quantity Demanded" across all international territories to guide the 2012 footprint.
High-Volume Engines: The Netherlands, EIRE, and Germany show the highest proven demand.
Expansion Logic: These regions represent "warm" markets where the infrastructure and customer appetite already exist, making them the lowest-risk targets for physical expansion or localized distribution centers.
🛠 Tech Stack & Methodology
Data Cleaning: Python/Pandas
Visualization:  Power BI and Matplotlib
Analysis Focus: Trend Forecasting, Customer Segmentation (RFM), and International Market Penetration.
